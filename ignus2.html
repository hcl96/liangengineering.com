Ignus II Liquid Rocket Engine

About Ignus II

Ignus II is a liquid rocket engine designed by SEDS UCSD to power a sounding rocket. Designed with modern engineering techniques and manufactured with metal 3D printing technology, Ignus II improves upon its predecessor the Ignus I in thrust, efficiency and reliability.
The engine uses Kerosene and Liquid Oxygen as its propellant and is printed out of Inconel-718. It consists of only two parts: the injector and combustion chamber.

The engine was hot-fired successfully on June 16th 2018 and is flight ready for future rockets.

The thermal protection system necessary to protect the engine from intense heating was designed and simulated in MATLAB.
